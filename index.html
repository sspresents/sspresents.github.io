About The Lorax (2012)

Based off the Dr. Seuss classic of the same name, The Lorax follows the adventures of a young boy, Ted, trying to impress the girl of his dreams. To do this, he needs to find and acquire a real, living tree—a mission that takes him to the aged Once-ler who, for some reason, knows why there are no more trees.

About Screen Sustain

Screen Sustain is an English 1102 course with the goal of fostering environmental discussions on the Georgia Tech campus. As the name suggests, we intend to initiate conversation by screening a film, for all films showcase complex environments, both in the physical setting(s) and characters’ cultures. Screen Sustain has particular interest in sustainability. Check out our class blog for posts about the toopic throughout the course here.
To this end, we have viewed and discussed several possible films, before finally selecting The Lorax (2012).

What is Ecocinema?

Defined broadly, ecocinema is any film that addresses the environment—either cultural or physical. The Lorax, then, qualifies as ecocinema.

First and foremost, The Lorax is ecocinema because it directly addresses sustainability. The Once-ler, an entrepreneur, sells “thneeds,” a product made from the Truffala Tree. As his business grows, the Once-ler must cut down progressively larger amounts of trees—which he does despite the titular Lorax’s protestations—until there are no more trees.

This newer adaptation includes more to this Dr. Seuss classic: a bustling, walled-in city; a second, less repentant entrepreneur; a romantic interest for the young Ted. The world of The Lorax is thus intended as a microcosm, representing our world: our willingness to ignore unpleasant realities, our love for the conveniences industrialism affords.
